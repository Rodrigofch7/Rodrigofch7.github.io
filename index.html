About

Methods meet place

I'm a Master's candidate in Computational Analysis and Public Policy at the University of Chicago, where I work at the intersection of econometrics and applied machine learning. My research asks how policy interventions — from privatization to police body cameras to data center construction — shape outcomes in the cities where they happen.

Before Chicago, I spent two years at Insper's Cities Lab in São Paulo, applying difference-in-differences and spatial regression to questions in Brazilian urban economics. That work produced a peer-reviewed publication and a forthcoming paper on policing. I'm currently a Research Assistant at the Mansueto Institute for Urban Innovation, building computer vision pipelines to digitize historical maps for large-scale urban research, and I spent the summer of 2026 with the World Bank's Development Impact Evaluation team in Washington, DC.

I'm a native Portuguese speaker, fluent in English, and conversational in Spanish — and I think in QGIS as often as I think in R.
